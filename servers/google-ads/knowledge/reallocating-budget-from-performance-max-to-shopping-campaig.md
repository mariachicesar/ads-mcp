## Topics

## Key Tactics
- Shift budget allocation from Performance Max toward Shopping campaigns when selling physical products; the hardware client increased Shopping spend from $1,000 to $10,500 (1000% increase) while reducing Pmax spend by 33%, resulting in $100,000 additional revenue.
- Monitor channel performance separately rather than relying solely on total spend increases; in this case, the 93% total budget increase was concentrated in Shopping, not distributed evenly across channels.
- Shopping campaigns may outperform Performance Max for e-commerce brands selling hardware and equipment with clear product feeds and pricing data.
- Test reducing Performance Max spend incrementally while monitoring ROAS; this client maintained similar absolute Pmax spend levels ($7K to $4.6K) while proving Shopping's superior efficiency for their product category.

## Summary
By reallocating 80% of Performance Max budget toward Shopping campaigns, a hardware e-commerce brand increased November revenue from $57,000 to $157,000 (175% growth) despite running identical promotional offers year-over-year. The strategy demonstrates that channel mix matters significantly—Shopping campaigns substantially outperformed Performance Max for this product category when given proportional budget increases.
