## Key Tactics
- Extract your brand search terms' average CPC from existing search campaigns and use this as your starting manual CPC bid for a new brand shopping campaign to establish a baseline.
- Analyze your return on ad spend (ROAS) for brand terms to determine if you should start with a more aggressive bid; if brand terms generate 1200%+ ROAS, consider starting at 1000–1500% target ROAS instead of conservative average CPC.
- Begin new brand shopping campaigns with manual CPC bidding to warm up the campaign and gather initial conversion data before transitioning to automated bidding.
- Once you've accumulated sufficient conversion volume and performance data, switch from manual CPC to Target ROAS (T-ROAS) bidding to optimize for return on spend.

## Summary
When launching a brand shopping campaign, start by reviewing your existing brand search term data to establish an appropriate initial bid—either at your average brand CPC (conservative approach) or at a target ROAS based on your brand term performance (aggressive approach). Use manual CPC bidding initially to gather data, then transition to Target ROAS once you have sufficient conversion volume to support automated optimization.
