## Key Tactics
- Analyze conversion volume and ROAS by campaign over the last 30 days to quickly identify underperforming campaigns that are "bleeding money" silently.
- Use time-based metrics when reviewing recent data to ensure accurate performance assessment of individual campaigns.
- Compare ROAS across campaigns to spot outliers: high-performing campaigns (like brand campaigns at 2000% ROAS) versus poor performers (like search campaigns at 0.79 ROAS).
- Flag search campaigns with unusually low conversion rates (0.05% or lower) and negative ROAS as immediate red flags requiring investigation or restructuring.
- Avoid running display alongside search campaigns in the same campaign structure, as this can muddy performance data and lead to poor results.
- Prioritize reviewing campaigns with minimal conversions in the period (e.g., 0.5 conversions) as they indicate either poor setup, poor targeting, or wasted budget.
- Conduct a quick high-level campaign-by-campaign review as the first step of any audit to identify structural issues before diving into granular optimization.

## Summary
This audit technique involves reviewing the last 30 days of campaign performance by conversion volume and ROAS to quickly spot campaigns wasting budget across an account. By comparing metrics like conversion rate and ROAS across campaigns, you can identify structural issues and underperforming campaigns that need immediate attention or restructuring.
