## Key Tactics
- Segment brand shopping campaigns separately from non-brand shopping and search campaigns to prevent brand traffic from skewing performance data of your core campaigns.
- Create a dedicated brand shopping campaign targeting your company's brand name (e.g., "bobscomputerparts") to capture branded shopping queries, since you cannot bid on keywords in Shopping campaigns like you can in Search.
- Implement brand protection as part of a larger campaign architecture that includes non-brand Shopping, non-brand Search, and Performance Max campaigns—keeping brand traffic isolated ensures accurate ROI measurement for each segment.
- Use brand shopping campaigns as part of a "feeder campaign" framework where brand protection runs separately from performance-focused campaigns to maintain data integrity and prevent brand terms from inflating non-brand campaign metrics.

## Summary
Brand shopping campaigns allow you to target your own brand name in Shopping ads by creating a separate, dedicated campaign structure. This isolation prevents brand traffic from distorting performance data across your non-brand campaigns (Search, Shopping, and Performance Max), enabling accurate measurement and optimization of each segment within a multi-campaign strategy.
