## Key Tactics
- Review Google's recommendations on a monthly basis, but don't automatically implement them; investigate each recommendation to determine if it aligns with your account strategy before accepting or dismissing.
- Dismiss recommendations that don't apply to your business or campaign goals to maintain a high optimization score, which reduces unsolicited outreach from Google representatives.
- Investigate specific recommendations that warrant deeper analysis—for example, if a campaign lacks sitelinks, examine why before deciding whether to add them.
- Maintain a high optimization score (aim for 95%+) as a strategic tactic to minimize interruptions and pressure from Google support representatives, giving you greater autonomy over your account management.

## Summary
A monthly review of Google Ads recommendations is important for account management, but most recommendations should be dismissed unless they address genuine gaps in your strategy. By maintaining a high optimization score through selective dismissal of irrelevant recommendations, you can reduce unwanted outreach from Google representatives and retain full control over your campaigns.
