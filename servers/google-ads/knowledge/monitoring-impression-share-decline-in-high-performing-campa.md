## Key Tactics
- Monitor impression share metrics weekly on your top-performing campaigns, specifically tracking both "search top impression share" and "absolute top impression share" to catch performance slippage early.
- When you notice a decline in impression share (e.g., dropping from 80% to 70% in a week), investigate the root causes: new competitor activity, budget constraints, or actual performance degradation.
- Use impression share data as an early warning system—declining share on campaigns that "crush" for you signals you may need to increase bids or budget to maintain competitive positioning.
- Pay special attention to Performance Max campaigns when analyzing impression share, as tracking this metric becomes more complex but remains critical for identifying optimization opportunities.

## Summary
Regularly reviewing impression share metrics on your best-performing campaigns helps you identify competitive threats and performance drops before they impact revenue. When impression share declines on a top campaign, investigate whether new competitors, budget limitations, or bid strategy changes are responsible, then adjust your bidding or budget accordingly to maintain your market position.
