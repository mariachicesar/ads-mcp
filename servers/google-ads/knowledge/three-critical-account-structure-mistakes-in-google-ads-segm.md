## Key Tactics
- Separate search and Performance Max campaigns entirely—they operate on fundamentally different mechanics and should never be mixed in the same campaign structure.
- Avoid mixing display and search networks within the same campaign; these channels have completely different user behaviors and performance patterns and require separate segmentation.
- Implement strict negative keyword strategies across all campaigns, especially when using broad match keywords, to prevent budget waste on irrelevant search terms.
- Use proper naming conventions consistently across all campaigns (e.g., "Brand," "Product Category," "Network Type") to maintain logical segmentation and make account audits and optimization easier.
- For brand campaigns targeting specific product brands, avoid using broad match keywords—use more restrictive match types to ensure you're bidding on relevant brand searches and preventing overlap with other brand campaigns.
- Segment campaigns by product category or business segment at the top level, then layer in additional segmentation by campaign type (brand, non-brand, performance max) to reduce keyword overlap and improve clarity.

## Summary
This video identifies three recurring structural mistakes in Google Ads accounts: poor campaign segmentation (especially mixing search with display and broad match keywords), inconsistent naming conventions that obscure campaign purpose, and the complete absence of negative keyword strategies. Fixing these foundational issues is critical before optimizing bidding, ad copy, or other tactical elements.
