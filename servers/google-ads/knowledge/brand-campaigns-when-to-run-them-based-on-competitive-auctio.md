## Key Tactics

- **Monitor Auction Insights for competitor bidding** — Check your Auction Insights report to see if competitors are actively bidding on your brand terms. If no competitors appear and incognito searches confirm this, you have a stronger case to test disabling brand ads.

- **Run brand ads when competitors bid on your name** — If competitors are bidding on your brand terms (e.g., "Casper mattress"), turning off your own brand ads risks losing top ad positions to competitor messaging that directly compares to your product.

- **Conduct incognito search tests** — Perform live searches of your brand terms in incognito mode to verify whether competitors are actually showing ads. This real-world data should inform your brand campaign decision.

- **Consider the competitive landscape in your decision** — The choice to run or disable brand campaigns should depend entirely on your specific market. High-competition categories (like mattresses) benefit from brand defense; low-competition markets may not need the spend.

- **Avoid blanket rules on brand campaigns** — Don't assume brand ads are always necessary or always wasteful. Let data from Auction Insights and manual searches guide your decision rather than industry dogma.

## Summary
Whether to run brand campaigns depends on competitive threats in your auction, not universal best practices. If competitors actively bid on your brand terms, running brand ads protects your top positions; if no competitors appear in Auction Insights and incognito searches, you can test disabling them to reduce spend.
