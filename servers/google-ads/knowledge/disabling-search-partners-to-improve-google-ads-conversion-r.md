## Key Tactics
- Review search partner performance every 90 days by segmenting network data; disable search partners if volume is low (under 100 clicks) or conversion rates significantly underperform Google Search, even if they generate some sales.
- Search partners often deliver lower-quality traffic and can confuse smart bidding algorithms (like Target ROAS); removing them allows the algorithm to focus optimization solely on higher-performing Google Search traffic.
- Only keep search partners enabled if your primary Google Search campaign is maxed out in volume and you need additional scale; for most accounts with healthy search volumes, they should be disabled.
- Compare conversion rates between Google Search and search partners side-by-side; if search partner conversion rates are materially lower (e.g., 1.8% vs 2.67%), the marginal sales often don't justify the added complexity.

## Summary
Search partners frequently deliver low-quality traffic and dilute smart bidding performance without meaningful volume gains. Review search partner performance every 90 days and disable them unless your primary Google Search campaign is fully saturated, allowing your bidding strategy to focus optimization on higher-converting channels.
