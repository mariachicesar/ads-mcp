## Key Tactics
- Schedule biweekly reviews of active ad tests to analyze performance data and identify winning variations, then pause underperforming ads.
- Maintain organized testing documentation (such as a testing sheet) that tracks what ad copy variations, landing pages, or other elements are currently being tested.
- If you're not currently running ad tests, treat it as a priority action item—implement a testing cadence to continuously improve ad performance.
- When reviewing multiple ads in a search campaign, look for major performance differences that indicate which ad copy resonates best with your audience.
- Use ad testing results to inform decisions about which creative elements to keep, pause, or iterate on in future campaigns.

## Summary
Regular ad testing is a critical maintenance task that should be reviewed biweekly to identify top-performing ad variations and pause underperformers. Whether you use a structured testing sheet or review tests directly in your campaign, analyzing ad performance data helps optimize your creative strategy. If you're not currently running ad tests, start implementing them immediately as a core part of account optimization.
