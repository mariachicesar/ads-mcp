## Key Tactics
- Use Auction Insights data to identify whether competitors are bidding on your branded keywords; if no competitors appear, brand ads may not be necessary for defensive purposes.
- Compare revenue and new customer acquisition metrics for identical four-week periods before and after pausing brand campaigns to quantify their actual impact.
- For seasonal businesses, consider pausing brand campaigns during low-demand periods (fall/winter) when search volume and competitor activity naturally decline.
- Evaluate brand campaign performance not just on revenue, but on new customer acquisition rates—if both metrics remain flat or improve after pausing, the spend may be better allocated elsewhere.
- Present data-driven analysis (actual revenue and customer acquisition comparisons) to stakeholders when recommending brand campaign changes to justify budget reallocation decisions.

## Summary
This video demonstrates how to objectively evaluate whether brand ads are necessary by analyzing auction insights data and comparing revenue/customer acquisition metrics before and after pausing brand campaigns. For this seasonal business with a unique name and no competitor bidding, shutting down brand ads during off-season months resulted in no revenue loss and actually increased new customer acquisition, suggesting the budget could be better deployed elsewhere.
