## Key Tactics
- Use the "View Asset Detail" button in Google Ads to access performance data for all ad assets (headlines, descriptions, images) within a responsive display ad.
- Identify low-performing descriptions and headlines in the asset performance overview, then remove them and test new variations to improve overall ad performance.
- Replace underperforming images with new creative and monitor performance over time as you accumulate additional data to validate improvements.
- Continuously feed the Google algorithm high-quality creative assets by iteratively testing and removing low-performers rather than relying solely on automation.
- Check back periodically on replaced assets to compare new performance against the old baseline and validate that your optimizations are working.

## Summary
While Google's algorithm optimizes responsive display ads automatically, you can significantly improve results by manually reviewing asset-level performance data, identifying underperforming headlines, descriptions, and images, and systematically replacing them with new variations. This hands-on approach to asset management helps feed the algorithm better creative and drives stronger ad performance over time.
