## Key Tactics
- Reallocate Performance Max budget to Shopping campaigns, which can deliver significantly higher ROAS; in the furniture example, Shopping spend increased 421% while Performance Max decreased 80%, resulting in 35% conversion value growth.
- Monitor channel-level spend distribution monthly to identify underperforming campaigns; the shift from $40K Performance Max to $9K freed up budget for higher-converting channels.
- Use year-over-year account comparisons to isolate the impact of budget optimization from external factors like seasonal promotions or deal variations.
- Focus on conversion value and revenue impact rather than spend alone; the client achieved $206K additional revenue while only increasing total spend by $14K (15% increase).
- Test reducing Performance Max budget allocation when Shopping campaigns show stronger conversion efficiency, as Performance Max may cannibalize higher-intent search and shopping traffic.

## Summary
By strategically reducing Performance Max spend by 80% and reallocating budget to Shopping campaigns, this furniture ecommerce brand achieved a 35% increase in conversion value and $206K additional revenue with only a 15% increase in total account spend. The case demonstrates that Performance Max is not always the optimal channel and that careful budget allocation across Google Ads channels—based on actual performance data—can dramatically improve return on ad spend.
