## Key Tactics
- Run a weekly performance check by filtering the last 7 days and comparing it to the previous period to identify spending anomalies and revenue trends before they compound into larger issues.
- Monitor cost-per-acquisition (ROAS) consistency week-to-week; if costs are increasing proportionally with revenue at similar efficiency, the account is pacing normally and scaling healthily.
- Use weekly performance data to identify which campaigns are underperforming or overperforming, then reallocate budget accordingly to high-performing campaigns that warrant additional investment.
- Establish a routine ritual of auditing account metrics every week to catch budget drift early; this preventative check prevents wasted spend and surfaces optimization opportunities before they impact your monthly results.

## Summary
A weekly performance audit—comparing the last 7 days to the prior period—helps you spot budget pacing issues and campaign performance anomalies before they become costly. By tracking whether cost increases align with revenue gains at consistent ROAS levels, you can confidently scale budgets to top performers and pull back from underperformers, turning data into actionable budget reallocation decisions.
