## Key Tactics
- **Analyze existing brand search data**: Review your current brand campaign search query reports to determine the average CPC for brand terms before launching a shopping campaign—this provides a data-backed starting point rather than guessing.
- **Start conservative with manual CPC**: Use manual CPC bidding set to your brand search average CPC when first launching a brand shopping campaign to warm up the campaign safely while gathering performance data.
- **Factor in ROAS potential**: Calculate your historical return on ad spend (ROAS) on brand terms; if you see strong returns (e.g., 1200%+ ROAS), you can justify starting with a higher target ROAS in your initial bid strategy.
- **Transition to Target ROAS after data accumulation**: Begin with manual CPC, collect conversion volume and performance data over time, then migrate to Target ROAS (T-ROAS) bidding to automate optimization at scale.
- **Use return on ad spend as a proxy for initial bid aggressiveness**: When determining how aggressive to set initial bids, reference your brand terms' ROAS; conservative operators can start at 1000–1200% target ROAS, while more aggressive bidders can start at 1500%+ ROAS.

## Summary
When launching a new brand shopping campaign, use your existing brand search campaign data to inform initial bid strategy. Start with manual CPC set to your average brand search CPC, then transition to Target ROAS bidding once you've accumulated sufficient conversion data and validated your expected return on ad spend.
